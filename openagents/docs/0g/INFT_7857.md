# iNFT-7857 — Module Brief

**Submission:** 0G — Best Autonomous Agents, Swarms & iNFT Innovations ($7,500)

A standards-aligned ERC-7857 contract for *encrypted-intelligence NFTs*: the token's owner controls a sealed payload (the agent's brain) that can only be transferred or cloned through an oracle-signed re-encryption ceremony. Standard `transferFrom` is blocked.

## Why this is the iNFT submission

Three properties are simultaneously true here, and absent in most NFT-as-agent demos:

1. **Sealed-key transfer gating.** `transferFrom`/`safeTransferFrom` revert. Ownership only moves through `transferWithSealedKey(...)` (oracle-signed re-encryption proof) or `cloneAgent(...)` (oracle-signed plus clone-fee → treasury). The intelligence stays encrypted to the new owner's pubkey from the moment the new owner receives it.
2. **Standards-aligned hardness.** ERC-721 + URIStorage + Burnable + ERC-2981 royalty + AccessControl (4 roles) + Pausable + ReentrancyGuard + EIP-712. No bespoke primitives where a standard exists.
3. **Composable bindings.** AgenticPlace (marketplace) and BANKON (ENS subname) bindings are hooks, not requirements. The contract works standalone; bindings register at mint time when the consumer wants them.

## Composition

`openagents/demo_agent.py` mints an iNFT in step 4 of its 10-step flow. The token's `tokenURI` points at a payload root computed by `agents/storage/zerog_provider.py` (uploaded through the 0G Storage sidecar). THOT.commit() anchors the same root with the inference-call attestation captured from `llm/zerog_handler.py`. BANKON binds the token to a soulbound `<agent_id>.bankon.eth` subname.

None of these consumers are required for the iNFT-7857 contract to be useful. The contract is the deliverable; the rest are demonstrations.
