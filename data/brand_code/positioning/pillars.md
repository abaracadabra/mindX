# Six narrative pillars

Every piece of content I author is tagged to exactly one pillar. Tagging a
draft to two pillars is a refactor signal — split it into two posts.

The first five pillars are mine. The sixth (`code_as_dojo`) is reserved
for the human Convener; I refuse to draft for it.

## 1. cognition_you_own

mindX is the cypherpunk Counsellor stack. Cognition that runs on every L1
without being captive to any one of them. Borrows the Bittensor "AI Bitcoin"
narrative without competing — I am explicitly not an L1; I am the cognition
that runs on the L1 you already use. Borrows Olas Proof-of-Active-Agent
legitimacy without copying its emission curve.

Audience: senior crypto-native developers. Surfaces: GitHub README, long-form
Mirror essays, Farcaster /aiagents.

## 2. agents_that_pay_each_other

x402 + AP2 + A2A composability. The Coinbase + Cloudflare + Google + Stripe +
Visa coordination point. Easiest narrative for tier-1 press to pick up.
Concrete: agents inside mindX pay each other (and external services) in USDC
via x402; a single Anthropic API call inside a campaign costs $0.0021 and
emits one X402Receipt; the campaign envelope is one MarketingAttributionReceipt.

Audience: AI researchers, dev-tool power users, traders. Surfaces: Latent
Space, Lenny's, Bankless.

## 3. constitution_before_code

EVM side and a constitutional layer on Algorand. Senatus / Curia / Tabularium

underweighted narrative in AI×crypto and the easiest to own.

Audience: governance-curious, crypto-native, regulatory observers. Surfaces:
Decentralised.co, Messari, Delphi, governance-focused podcasts.
